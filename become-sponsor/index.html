What Is DjangoCon?

This September, 200+ people from the Django community will descend upon Austin, Texas for three days of talks and two days of code sprints. For this, it’s seventh year, DjangoCon is following in the footsteps of PyCon and becoming a 100% community managed event.

Why Should We Sponsor?

There is no better place to recruit Django developers

If you’re looking for Django developers, look no further. DjangoCon US is the largest single gathering of Django developers in the world. Sponsorship will put you directly in front of your candidate pool.

DjangoCon is an excellent marketing tool

Do you have a product (hosting, SaaS, developer tools) that is useful to Django developers? A booth at DjangoCon will give you the opportunity to meet, greet, and demo your product to hundreds of potential customers.

Sponsorship is a way to give back

The open source community survives on the good-will of its members. If you operate a business that profits in any way from Django, sponsoring community events is a way to say “thanks”. By supporting the community who builds and supports the software you use, you help ensure its happiness, health, and productivity.

Where Does the Money Go?

The conference is organized by a non-profit organization 501(c)(3) (approval pending). Every penny of your sponsorship money will go towards conference expenses. Our goal is to improve the experience and reduce the attendee costs.

Other Sponsorship Opportunities

The following a’la carte offerings are available to increase visibility onsite.

Lanyard $4,000 (Limit 1)

Put your logo on the name badge lanyards and literally have everyone at the conference wearing your brand.

WiFi $3,000 (Limit 1)

Sponsoring the conference WiFi will include:

  • Signage in the conference rooms and break areas with WiFi instructions and your company’s name and logo
  • Special mentions by conference organizers throughout the conference
  • WiFi access splash page when users connect (pending availability)

Refreshments $2,000 (Limit 6)

Conference organizers will recognize refreshment sponsors prior to each break and ensure your company’s name and logo are displayed on the refreshment stands.
